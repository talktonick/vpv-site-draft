On this page

The constraint was never the platform

Quip came to us with a four-month window and a store that had accumulated years of logic — redirects, metafields, and merchandising rules nobody fully remembered but everybody depended on. The brief looked like a replatform. The real work was an edit.

What we chose to carry forward

We mapped the existing system and decided, deliberately, what earned its place in the new build. A migration is a forcing function for editing — and treating it that way is what kept the timeline honest.

A theme built to be merchandised

The new system put control in the marketing team's hands: drag-and-drop collection rules, a flexible PDP, and a subscription flow built for retention from day one — no developer in the loop for everyday updates.

The best migration is the one your customers never notice — until everything is suddenly faster.
